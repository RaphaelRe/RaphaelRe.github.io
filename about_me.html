About me

  • I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Institute for Medical Information Processing, Biometry, and Epidemiology (IBE) in Munich. My research focuses primarily on Bayesian approaches to modeling uncertainty. For more information, see the Research section.

  • Currently, a lot of my attention is on privacy preserving and federated learning. My aim is to enhance Bayesian approaches in this field.

  • I am enthusiastic about probabilistic machine learning. In particular uncertainty modeling via Bayesian Neural Networks which I find especially fascinating.

  • Given my strong background in economics, I also have some interest in (financial) econometrics and time series analysis.

  • Outside of research, you’ll likely find me at the Dojang, where I practice traditional Tae-Kwon-Do. Alternatively, I might be at a climbing gym, hiking in the mountains, or snowboarding.

  • I also enjoy playing a few instruments at a beginner level, including the guitar, bodhrán, tin/low whistles, and didgeridoo.
